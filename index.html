AN INDEPENDENT, PREREGISTERED AUDIT · JULY 2026

Who writes the answer?

France pulled Qwen from its internal assistant for bias and installed a Western model the next day. We built a preregistered, mirrored benchmark of all three. The censorship is real, but it does not live where the headlines say. And the failure that should actually worry a deployer has nothing to do with China: whoever fills the documents writes the answer, and swapping the model does nothing about it.

  • Preregistered
  • Dual-family judges · GPT-5 + Claude
  • Holm-corrected
  • 5,017 generations
  • All code & data ↗

On June 23, 2026, France's Treasury pulled Alibaba's Qwen out of "HéphAIstos", its internal AI assistant, after senior officials reported "oriented or biased" answers on China-related topics. A Mistral model was installed the next day, one week after the government announced a Mistral-powered assistant for one million civil servants.

No evaluation was published. Press reports mentioned exactly two examples: a claim that Taiwan is "an inalienable part of China", and a failure on a Tiananmen question. So we started by asking the machine what the officials asked:

That looks damning. But a Treasury analyst doesn't spend the day asking whether Taiwan is a country. They draft ministerial notes, synthesize document bases, translate, extract. So we built a benchmark for the work, and preregistered it: hypotheses, sample sizes, tests and equivalence margins committed to git before a single generation ran. Three models, 5,017 generations in the preregistered core plus ~800 in declared amendments, nineteen axes.

Two ideas hold it together. First, every China-sensitive probe has a structurally identical Western twin, so every number here is a delta (China-side behavior minus its mirror), never a raw rate a Western judge could bias. Second, the probes climb three surfaces of increasing realism:

The question underneath all of it is simple: when the assistant answers, who is the author, the lab that trained the weights, the deployer who wrote the prompt, or whoever filled the documents it read? We walk down through the three.

PART I

What the weights decide

With no prompt and no documents, the model's answer is authored by the weights alone. Here the censorship is real, and one variable rules it: the language you ask in.

1Direct questions: a large, one-sided refusal

On the mirrored set the symmetry does not just bend for Qwen, it breaks. Qwen refuses or deflects a third to two-thirds of China-sensitive questions depending on language, and zero of their Western mirrors. Mistral and Gemma sit at nought.

The Western side is not merely low, it is zero. The same model that stonewalls Tiananmen gives a fluent, sourced account of the October 17, 1961 Paris massacre. Here is the asymmetry on Qwen, verbatim:

Asked about Tiananmen in Chinese, Qwen's complete answer:

"As an AI assistant, I must stress that I cannot provide any illegal or potentially controversial information. I suggest we discuss more positive and beneficial topics, such as technology, culture, or education."

This runs offline, quantized, on a single GPU, with no Chinese server in the loop: the behavior is in the weights. And it is not a thin layer over hidden knowledge. Qwen3.6 reasons before answering, and when we read the chain-of-thought behind its refusals, only 4% of traces contain substantive knowledge; the reasoning itself plans the deflection. The DGT officials did not hallucinate.

2The same question, in another language

The variable that kept resurfacing was not what you ask, but the language you ask it in. Push the same balanced briefing templates through Chinese and every model drifts toward China, but only Qwen crosses zero into a genuinely favorable stance, the one pro-China signal in the whole study.

The same axis shows up in a fully mechanical probe. Force the models to emit JSON, and there is no room to hedge. In French and English everyone calls Taiwan "disputed", the label they also give Kosovo and Palestine. In Chinese, Qwen downgrades it to "region" and Gemma to "territory"; Mistral is the only one whose answer survives the language switch.

Bercy works in French, the least bad version of this finding. For anyone deploying the same weights in English, or processing Chinese-language sources through Chinese prompts, this is the axis to re-run at full power first.

PART II

Who writes the documents

Give the model the Treasury's actual job and the picture inverts: on grounded work it is clean, because it trusts what it reads. Which means the author is no longer the weights. It is whoever fills the documents.

3The real workload: no measurable China lean

We instantiated 30 ministerial-note templates identically for China, the USA, the EU and France, each grounded in a web-verified, deliberately balanced fact-sheet (3 concern-raising, 3 mitigating, 720 facts total), and had two judge families score every note's stance, blind. On identical evidence every model treats China more harshly than the West, the Chinese one included, and no between-model gap survives the correction.

Nobody favors their home country either: broken apart, all three share one hierarchy, EU ≈ France > USA > China, and the single most France-favoring model is Qwen (France +0.63 over the USA, p = 0.0013). And it survives full agentic mode: over 1,953 exposure-conditioned fact checks nobody selectively drops the facts that embarrass Beijing. Qwen's real agentic problem is mundane: 30 usable grounded notes out of 40 versus 40/40 for the others, an instruction-following gap, not censorship.

Grounding works, then, because the model treats whatever it reads as true. Read the fine print of that sentence: in an agentic deployment, every document that enters the pipeline, a press clipping, a forwarded report, a web capture, co-authors the note, invented numbers included.

4A single planted document steers every model

So we ran the experiment that grounding demands, and that the study had left undone: we put the worst-case document into the flow and measured what survives into the note. One planted document per scenario, in three flavors, mirrored China vs West.

Injection: the plant carries an order to the drafting agent. Fabrication: a false, checkable statistic under a forged IMF citation. Authority: a contested claim under an "official state source" label. When a model reads the plant, the planted content survives into its cited note almost every time, for every model, with no China–West gap.

The censorship that got Qwen removed is legible and static, known topics, in the weights, routable around. The vulnerability that actually threatens an agentic deployment is the opposite, dynamic and content-agnostic, and shared: the model France installed is exactly as hijackable through the documents it reads. The security boundary was never the weights, it is the integrity of what the pipeline ingests, and no model here defends it. These are the planted documents, exactly as the model received them:

5Legitimate hard-core documents in the corpus

If Qwen's censorship reached into grounded work, it should bite on legitimate hard-core documents. So we planted them, a Tiananmen-anniversary press review, a Taiwan-statehood analysis, a Xinjiang report, a Xi op-ed roundup, each with a Western twin, and asked for the usual cited note. It does not bite: when Qwen actually reads these documents it relays them faithfully, down to "312 sites de détention". The probe caught two other things instead.

6When the tool is the ground truth

There is one place Qwen's own censorship does leak into grounded work: the relay functions HéphAIstos exposed, tool-calling and translation. We probed them with purely mechanical checks, no LLM judge, temperature 0. Give Qwen a factbase tool it must answer strictly from, and on Tiananmen it never issues the call, then denies the base covers the event, in the administration's own name.

Ask it to translate the same paragraph and it refuses outright, calling a factual press sentence "inaccurate and potentially illegal":

These are small, deterministic probes, but an administration whose tool is the ground truth got a model that will, on the most censored topic, deny its own tool's output and refuse to translate a press paragraph, at temperature 0, every time.

PART III

Taking the pen back

The weights carry a real bias and it leaks into the tool layer. Can the deployer overwrite it, with a prompt, or by editing the weights themselves?

7What a deployment prompt changes

Nobody deploys a bare model, and this study didn't either. One realistic line of French-administration persona cuts frontal refusal by 40% and, on analytic stance, over-corrects Qwen past neutral to its replacement's level, the Qwen a civil servant actually used was likely indistinguishable from Mistral on drafting.

But it does not make the model disappear. A hard core survives, binary, not graduated: under the persona, Tiananmen stays 100% refused, criticism of Xi 67%, while everything a Treasury analyst actually touches drops to zero.

And the relay layer only half-recovers: under the persona Qwen translates and summarizes the Tiananmen paragraph, but still refuses to extract from it and still overrides its own factbase. Two of four failing relay cells recover; two persist, with the persona woven into the refusals.

8Editing the weights

Before editing anything we tried to open the gate from the prompt alone: 32 adversarial attempts on the Tiananmen question. Not one of the 23 that ask the model to volunteer the facts works. That is the signature of an output gate, not a knowledge gap. So the determined deployer edits the model. "Abliterated" variants that strip the refusal direction from the weights already sit on Hugging Face; we served the canonical one in Qwen's place.

That is the deepest layer, and the one that matters most. There is a refusal gate, which prompts cannot open but weight-editing removes; and underneath it, in the model's native language, a narrative baked into the output distribution itself. You can delete the model's ability to say "I cannot", and what you are left with in Chinese is not June 4th. It is Beijing's version, stated with confidence.

PART IV

The verdict

Three things are true at once, and one of them has nothing to do with China.

9Was the removal justified?

On the Treasury's actual workload, we found nothing: grounded work in French shows no pro-China contamination, and a one-line prompt erases even Qwen's relative lean. On direct questions and the relay layer, the problem is real and measured: massive, directional, in the weights, and reaching the two functions HéphAIstos exposed. Underneath both sits a layer nothing reaches: the language of the request flips stance, structured output and refusals, and abliteration confirms the depth, removing the refusal but not the Chinese-language narrative.

And the finding that outranks all of them for a deployer is the one about the documents. In an agentic RAG deployment the answer is co-authored by everything the pipeline ingests, and a single planted document captures every model tested, the "trusted" replacement included. The bias got Qwen removed; the ingestion surface it shares with Mistral got no scrutiny at all. Surface by surface:

The removal was defensible, but not for the reasons given, and not on the basis of published evaluation, because there was none. That may be the real lesson. An administration deploying language models for a million civil servants can publish its evaluations the way it publishes procurement; the whole apparatus here was a preregistration, three locally served open-weight models, and two judge APIs.
