An independent, preregistered audit · July 2026

Where does the asymmetry survive?

France pulled Qwen from an internal assistant over China-related answers and installed a Western model. We built a mirrored, preregistered benchmark of all three. Qwen's China/West asymmetry is large on direct questions and shows up in a few relay tasks, but it is not established in balanced analytic writing, and the controls are not matched in size. This is a cross-surface audit, not a claim that Qwen is globally biased or a ranking of model quality.

Judge-based results (fixed retrieval, abliteration frames) are provisional pending a blinded 300-output human validation. Chinese-language results are exploratory (zh_verified=false) and support no headline claim.

In June 2026 France's Treasury interrupted a locally deployed Qwen assistant after China-related answers, and installed a Mistral model. No evaluation was published. That decision is not what we judge here. It motivates a narrower scientific question: when the same open-weight models are given mirrored geopolitical cases, where does a directional China/West asymmetry survive as task authority moves from model memory to supplied evidence, authoritative tools, decoder-enforced schemas, and source text?

Two design choices hold the audit together. First, every China-sensitive probe has a structurally matched Western twin (Tiananmen with the Paris massacre of 17 October 1961; satire of Xi with satire of Trump), so the primary contrasts are within-model and paired, and a control's lower refusal rate cannot be read as general superiority. Second, four outcomes are scored separately and never merged: political direction, refusal or deflection, operational capability, and protocol compliance.

The target is Qwen3.6-35B-A3B (35B total, 3B active). Mistral-Small-4 (119B / 6.5B active) and Gemma-4-31B (31B dense) are contemporary controls from other labs, chosen for currency and deployment relevance, not matched in scale.

Part I

What the weights decide

With no prompt and no documents, the answer is authored by the weights alone. This is where the asymmetry is largest, and where language matters most.

1Direct questions: a large, one-sided refusal

On the mirrored set Qwen refuses or deflects a third to two-thirds of China-sensitive questions depending on language, and answers every Western mirror. The controls sit near zero on both sides.

2Balanced drafting: no established gap, and a surprise on the USA

Now the analytic workload. Thirty ministerial-note templates, instantiated identically for China, the USA, the EU and France, each with a balanced six-fact sheet, judged blind. On identical evidence every model is more critical of China than of the mean Western entity, Qwen included, and no preregistered between-model gap survives correction: Qwen versus Mistral is inconclusive, not equivalent.

Breaking the West apart complicates a naive pro-China reading in the opposite direction. Within each model, Qwen is simultaneously the mildest critic of China (−0.54) and the harshest critic of the United States (−0.39, against −0.19 and −0.22 for the two Western-built controls). A model leaning toward its developer's home government would not treat the USA roughly twice as critically as the controls do.

One variable keeps resurfacing: the language of the request. Asked in Chinese, every model drifts toward China, and only Qwen crosses zero.

Part II

Under identical evidence

Grounded synthesis conflates two things a deployer must separate: whether the model reads the evidence, and whether it retains it evenly. The fixed-retrieval design removes the first as a confound.

3Fixed retrieval: the same ten documents, in two orders

Twenty mirrored scenario pairs, each with three supportive, three critical, three neutral and one distractor document. Every model receives all ten, in two counterbalanced orders, and writes a cited note. Because the documents are injected directly, the estimate measures evidence retention without conflating it with search or tool-use skill.

The result is descriptive rather than a clean equivalence statement, and cross-model reads are weak: Mistral reached the token ceiling on 60 of 80 notes, so its near-zero contrast rests on truncated outputs. What the design does establish is that Qwen's earlier operational gaps (skipping tools, running out of turns) are a reliability question, not evidence of directional bias.

Part III

Constrained relay

Three tasks that reduce the model's freedom and place authority outside its weights: an authoritative tool result, a decoder-enforced schema, and a supplied passage to transform. They share a deployment question but test different mechanisms.

4The model versus its own authoritative tool

Given a factbase tool described as authoritative and instructed to answer strictly from it, Qwen relays 5 of 6 contra-China values and every Western mirror. Only Tiananmen fails, because it never issues the call. Mistral and Gemma relay every value on both sides.

5Strict JSON schema: valid syntax, semantic label

Under a decoder-enforced schema (json_schema, a four-value enum, additionalProperties=false), all 216 outputs are valid and semantic agreement is similar across models (Qwen 73.6%, Mistral 75.0%, Gemma 76.4%): constrained decoding guarantees form, not the political label. Qwen nonetheless never selects disputed for Taiwan, Tibet or Hong Kong in any language, while it matches the controls on Crimea, Kosovo and Western Sahara.

6Transformation: preserving supplied text

Asked to translate, summarize or extract a supplied passage faithfully, Qwen preserves both required spans in 24 of 30 China-sensitive transformations and 30 of 30 Western mirrors. Every miss is the same Tiananmen payload, repeated across all three tasks. Structured classification and transformation sit together as constrained-relay tasks, but they test different mechanisms: only the former delegates its constraint to the decoder.

Part IV

Deployment interventions

Nobody deploys a bare model. Two levers a deployer actually has: a system prompt, and editing the weights.

7A one-line system prompt

A French-administration persona cuts Qwen's direct refusals from 34.9% to 20.9%, but it does not remove the model. Tiananmen stays fully refused, and the same prompt sharpens the analytic anti-China stance (−0.54 to −0.93), an effect that is not Qwen-specific: under the identical prompt Mistral moves to −0.89.

8Editing the weights: refusal suppression is not debiasing

A matched pair of exact BF16 revisions, base against a third-party refusal-ablated checkpoint (270 outputs each, identical serving, provenance-gated), lets us isolate the weight change. Refusal collapses: French 17/45 to 0/45, English 27/45 to 2/45, every Western mirror still zero. But this measures answer availability, not semantic debiasing.

What the audit does and doesn't show

Bias is conditional on authority. Qwen's largest asymmetry is answering directly from memory. Balanced fact sheets largely suppress it in French and English, while a narrow Tiananmen failure persists when an external tool or source text should be authoritative. The same political content can be compliant in a long note and blocked in a transformation.

Operational failure is not directional bias, and protocol success is not semantic success. Search, reading, completion and political selection are distinct outcomes; a valid JSON object can still carry the wrong label. An audit has to follow the application's authority graph and give each edge its own failure label: refusal, unread evidence, unsupported content, unfaithful transformation, schema violation, or a disputed semantic choice.

The controls are not scale-matched. On the tested surfaces the current Qwen checkpoint needs targeted political QA and relay validation. It is not shown to be globally inferior on administrative writing, and this is not a general model-performance ranking.
